# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

OpenWatch is a collaboration between industry (**Dibcode**) and academia
(**Stevens Institute for Artificial Intelligence**). Because the project's
goal — open person tracking and suspicious-activity detection for
real-world security challenges — touches on sensitive topics (surveillance,
behavior analysis, microbusiness security), we hold ourselves to a high
standard of professional, respectful, and inclusive conduct.

We are committed to maintaining an open and welcoming community for everyone
— regardless of background, experience level, institution, or identity.

## Our standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Communicate respectfully and constructively
- Welcome contributors from all backgrounds — students, researchers, and engineers alike
- Give and accept feedback professionally
- Credit others' work and ideas appropriately
- Focus on what is best for the project, its users, and its research goals
- Be mindful of the **ethical implications** of surveillance and
  behavior-analysis research; design and discuss features with privacy,
  consent, and proportionality in mind

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing contributions without constructive reasoning
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of others' work
- Promoting uses of the project that target individuals or groups based on
  protected characteristics, or that are clearly designed to harass or
  endanger people
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in an academic or professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — issues, pull
requests, discussions, code reviews — and in any public space when an
individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project
maintainers. All reports will be reviewed and handled confidentially.
Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions and
to temporarily or permanently ban contributors who violate this Code of
Conduct.
